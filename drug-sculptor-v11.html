SMILES

SMILES (Simplified Molecular-Input Line-Entry System) encodes molecular structure as a string. Example: CC(=O)Oc1ccccc1C(=O)O = Aspirin. Type or paste any valid SMILES to visualise and analyse.

Properties Guide

MWMolecular weight — Lipinski rule: ≤500 Da
LogPLipophilicity — Lipinski rule: ≤5. High LogP → poor solubility, high clearance
HBD/HBAH-bond donors/acceptors — Ro5: HBD ≤5, HBA ≤10
TPSATopological polar surface area — ≤140 Å² for oral absorption; ≤90 Å² for CNS
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (0–1). >0.6 = drug-like
RotBondsRotatable bonds — ≤10 preferred for oral bioavailability
